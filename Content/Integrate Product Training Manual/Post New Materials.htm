1.3 Post New Materials

A Post Process record is automatically created for the process once activated. Multiple Post Process records can be configured for a single process, with each process post record having different post settings.

There are two ways to post data to SAP:

  • Post – Immediately posts data in the foreground and prompts for Back End Authentication.
  • Background Post – Schedules a post to run at a later date and time, and in the background. There are two primary reasons to post in the background:
  • There is too much data to post in the foreground without timing out the session.
  • To avoid putting too much stress on the system.

NOTE:Once the Process is ready to post, it needs to be activated. If any changes need to be made it must be deactivated.

To activate and post the new materials:

  1. Click Post on the Process page for the process, the Process Post displays in the Dependent pane.
  1. Click Post on the Process Post page in the Dependent pane, a message displays.
  1. Click the OK button.

NOTE:If the session is still active from the previous SAP access, SAP credentials are not required to be entered.

NOTE:A green message displays in the upper right corner indicating 10 records were posted successfully. This does not necessarily mean all records actually posted, it indicates the post successfully connected to SAP. To verify the success of a post view messages, go to Monitor post.
